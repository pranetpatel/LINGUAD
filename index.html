How it works

The loop that produces fluency

Gamified apps reward tapping. Fluency comes from production — speaking and being understood. Every Lingua session runs this loop, and every turn updates your skills.

STEP 1

Speak

Pick a scenario — ordering food, a job interview, a dragon adventure. The mic opens itself. Just talk.

STEP 2

Your tutor answers

Mila leads the scene, plays every character, and stretches your level — out loud, in a human voice.

STEP 3

Debrief

Corrections wait until the end so the conversation flows. Then practice each fix aloud, scored word by word.

STEP 4

Skills move

Speaking, comprehension, vocabulary, and grammar update live — progress you can see, not a gem count.

Questions

Fair questions, straight answers

Why not just use ChatGPT's voice mode?

You can, and it's a fine generalist. Lingua is a tutor: it remembers your level and your error history, scores pronunciation word by word, runs a curriculum, keeps kid conversations inside hard safety rails, and gives parents visibility. None of that exists in a general chatbot.

Does it really work for a 6-year-old?

Kids get their own interface: bigger buttons, a fox or bunny tutor with a kid voice, story time, and stars. One tap starts the whole conversation, and the mic re-opens by itself after the tutor speaks — designed so an early reader can use it alone.

What languages are available?

English and Spanish at launch, in both directions. French, German, and Japanese are next — languages are content modules in our architecture, so the roadmap moves quickly.

What do you do with my child's data?

As little as possible. No ads, no data sales, sharing features off by default and gated behind a parent PIN, and milestone clips are voice-over-animation — we never record or request your child's face.
